
=== MACRONUTRIENTS ===
Carbohydrates: Main energy source. 4 calories per gram. Found in grains, fruits, vegetables. Daily intake: 45-65% of total calories.
Proteins: Build and repair tissues. 4 calories per gram. Found in meat, eggs, legumes, dairy. Daily intake: 10-35% of calories. Adults need 0.8g per kg body weight.
Fats: Energy storage and hormone production. 9 calories per gram. Healthy fats from avocados, nuts, olive oil. Daily intake: 20-35% of calories.
Fiber: Aids digestion, prevents constipation. Found in whole grains, fruits, vegetables. Adults need 25-38g per day.
Water: Essential for all body functions. Adults need 2-3 liters per day.

=== MICRONUTRIENTS - VITAMINS ===
Vitamin A: Eye health, immune function. Sources: carrots, sweet potatoes, spinach, liver. Daily intake: 700-900 mcg.
Vitamin B1 (Thiamine): Energy metabolism. Sources: whole grains, legumes, nuts. Daily intake: 1.1-1.2 mg.
Vitamin B2 (Riboflavin): Energy production. Sources: dairy, eggs, leafy greens. Daily intake: 1.1-1.3 mg.
Vitamin B3 (Niacin): DNA repair, metabolism. Sources: meat, fish, peanuts. Daily intake: 14-16 mg.
Vitamin B6: Protein metabolism, brain function. Sources: poultry, fish, potatoes, bananas. Daily intake: 1.3-1.7 mg.
Vitamin B12: Nerve function, red blood cells. Sources: meat, dairy, eggs, fortified foods. Daily intake: 2.4 mcg.
Vitamin C: Antioxidant, immune support, collagen synthesis. Sources: citrus fruits, bell peppers, broccoli, strawberries. Daily intake: 75-90 mg.
Vitamin D: Calcium absorption, bone health, immune function. Sources: sunlight, fatty fish, fortified dairy. Daily intake: 600-800 IU.
Vitamin E: Antioxidant, skin health. Sources: nuts, seeds, vegetable oils, leafy greens. Daily intake: 15 mg.
Vitamin K: Blood clotting, bone health. Sources: leafy greens, broccoli, fermented foods. Daily intake: 90-120 mcg.
Folate (B9): Cell growth, DNA synthesis. Sources: leafy greens, legumes, fortified foods. Daily intake: 400 mcg.

=== MICRONUTRIENTS - MINERALS ===
Calcium: Bone and teeth health, muscle function. Sources: dairy, fortified plant milks, leafy greens, tofu. Daily intake: 1000-1200 mg.
Iron: Oxygen transport in blood. Sources: red meat, spinach, lentils, fortified cereals. Daily intake: 8-18 mg.
Magnesium: Muscle and nerve function, energy production. Sources: nuts, seeds, whole grains, leafy greens. Daily intake: 310-420 mg.
Potassium: Heart function, blood pressure regulation. Sources: bananas, potatoes, avocados, beans. Daily intake: 2600-3400 mg.
Zinc: Immune function, wound healing. Sources: meat, shellfish, legumes, nuts. Daily intake: 8-11 mg.
Selenium: Antioxidant, thyroid function. Sources: Brazil nuts, seafood, meat. Daily intake: 55 mcg.
Sodium: Fluid balance, nerve function. Sources: table salt, processed foods. Daily intake: less than 2300 mg.
Phosphorus: Bone health, energy metabolism. Sources: dairy, meat, fish, nuts. Daily intake: 700 mg.
Iodine: Thyroid hormone production. Sources: iodized salt, seafood, dairy. Daily intake: 150 mcg.

=== DIABETES-FRIENDLY DIET ===
Diabetes management focuses on controlling blood sugar levels through diet.
Low glycemic index foods: lentils, chickpeas, most vegetables, whole grains, berries.
Foods to eat: Non-starchy vegetables (spinach, broccoli, cauliflower), whole grains (quinoa, barley), lean proteins (fish, chicken, tofu), legumes, nuts and seeds, low-fat dairy.
Foods to avoid: Sugary drinks, white bread, white rice, pastries, candy, processed snacks, fruit juices.
Meal timing: Eat at consistent times, avoid skipping meals. 3 main meals + 2 small snacks per day.
Portion control: Use the plate method - half plate vegetables, quarter plate lean protein, quarter plate whole grains.
Carbohydrate counting: Aim for 45-60g carbs per meal for most adults with diabetes.
Fiber is key: Slows glucose absorption. Aim for 25-35g fiber per day.
Hydration: Water is best. Avoid sugary drinks. Limit alcohol.

=== HEART-HEALTHY DIET ===
Mediterranean diet is recommended for heart health.
Key principles: Plenty of fruits and vegetables, whole grains over refined grains, lean proteins, healthy fats.
Healthy fats: Olive oil, avocados, nuts, fatty fish (salmon, mackerel, sardines).
Foods to eat: Salmon, sardines, mackerel, walnuts, flaxseeds, chia seeds, olive oil, avocados, oats, berries, dark leafy greens, tomatoes, garlic, legumes.
Foods to limit: Saturated fats (red meat, full-fat dairy), trans fats (fried foods, margarine), sodium (less than 2300mg/day), added sugars, alcohol.
Omega-3 fatty acids: Reduce inflammation and triglycerides. Found in fatty fish, walnuts, flaxseeds.
Potassium-rich foods: Help lower blood pressure. Bananas, potatoes, avocados, spinach.
DASH diet: Dietary Approaches to Stop Hypertension - focus on fruits, vegetables, whole grains, low-fat dairy.

=== WEIGHT LOSS DIET ===
Sustainable weight loss: 0.5-1 kg per week is healthy and sustainable.
Caloric deficit: 500-750 calories below maintenance per day for safe weight loss.
High protein diet: Increases satiety, preserves muscle mass. Aim for 1.2-1.6g protein per kg body weight.
Fiber-rich foods: Keep you full longer. Vegetables, fruits, legumes, whole grains.
Meal planning: Plan meals in advance to avoid impulsive eating.
Hydration: Drink water before meals. Sometimes thirst is mistaken for hunger.
Foods to prioritize: Vegetables, lean proteins, whole grains, fruits, legumes.
Foods to limit: Processed foods, sugary drinks, alcohol, fried foods, high-calorie snacks.
Exercise combination: Combine diet with 150+ minutes of moderate exercise per week.
Intermittent fasting: 16:8 method (eat within 8-hour window) can help reduce calorie intake.

=== MUSCLE BUILDING DIET ===
Caloric surplus: 300-500 calories above maintenance for lean muscle gain.
Protein: Critical for muscle synthesis. Aim for 1.6-2.2g per kg body weight. 
Best protein sources: Chicken breast, eggs, Greek yogurt, cottage cheese, salmon, tuna, lean beef, whey protein.
Carbohydrates: Fuel for workouts. Focus on complex carbs - oats, brown rice, sweet potatoes, quinoa.
Pre-workout meal: Carbs + protein 1-2 hours before. Example: oats with protein powder.
Post-workout meal: Protein + carbs within 30 minutes. Critical for muscle recovery. Example: chicken with rice.
Healthy fats: Support hormone production. Avocados, nuts, olive oil.
Creatine: One of few supplements with strong evidence. 3-5g per day.
Hydration: Extra important during intense training. 3+ liters per day.

=== AGE-SPECIFIC NUTRITION ===
Children (2-12 years): Need nutrient-dense foods. Higher protein and calcium per kg body weight. Avoid processed foods, excess sugar. Iron-rich foods for brain development.
Teenagers (13-18 years): High calcium (1300mg) for bone development. Iron needs increase especially for girls. Protein for growth. Adequate calories for energy.
Adults (19-50 years): Balanced macronutrients. Maintain healthy weight. Iron needs for women of childbearing age.
Adults (51-70 years): Calcium 1200mg, Vitamin D 800IU, B12 supplementation may be needed. Reduce sodium. Adequate protein to prevent muscle loss.
Seniors (70+): Higher protein needs to prevent sarcopenia. Calcium and Vitamin D critical. B12, folate important. Adequate hydration (may not feel thirst).

=== PREGNANCY NUTRITION ===
Folic acid: 600 mcg/day to prevent neural tube defects. Before conception too.
Iron: 27 mg/day. Lean red meat, spinach, fortified cereals.
Calcium: 1000 mg/day. Dairy, fortified plant milks, leafy greens.
Protein: Additional 25g per day above normal needs.
DHA omega-3: Brain development of baby. Fatty fish (low mercury), flaxseeds, DHA supplements.
Foods to avoid: Raw fish, high-mercury fish, unpasteurized dairy, raw eggs, alcohol.
Weight gain: 11-16 kg for normal BMI pre-pregnancy women.

=== SPORTS NUTRITION ===
Pre-exercise: Carbohydrate-rich meal 3-4 hours before. Light carb snack 30-60 minutes before.
During exercise (>60 min): 30-60g carbs per hour. Sports drinks, gels, or bananas.
Post-exercise: 20-40g protein within 30 minutes. Carbohydrates to replenish glycogen.
Hydration: 500ml water 2 hours before. 150-250ml every 15-20 minutes during exercise. Rehydrate after.
Electrolytes: Sodium, potassium, magnesium lost in sweat. Sports drinks or food sources.

=== FOOD ALLERGIES AND INTOLERANCES ===
Gluten intolerance/Celiac: Avoid wheat, barley, rye. Safe grains: rice, quinoa, oats (certified GF), corn, millet.
Lactose intolerance: Avoid dairy or use lactase enzyme. Alternatives: almond milk, soy milk, oat milk, coconut milk.
Nut allergy: Avoid all tree nuts and peanuts if severe. Read food labels carefully.
Egg allergy: Avoid eggs and egg products. Alternatives in cooking: flaxseed meal, chia seeds, applesauce.
Soy allergy: Avoid soy products. Common in processed foods - read labels.
Fish/Shellfish allergy: Avoid all seafood. Get omega-3 from flaxseeds, walnuts, algae supplements.

=== SUPPLEMENTS ===
Vitamin D: Many people are deficient, especially those with limited sun exposure. 1000-4000 IU/day.
Omega-3: If not eating fatty fish 2x/week. 1-2g EPA+DHA per day.
B12: Essential for vegetarians and vegans. 1000 mcg/day or 250 mcg/day consistently.
Iron: Only supplement if deficient (blood test). Excess iron is harmful.
Calcium: If diet is consistently low. Calcium citrate better absorbed than calcium carbonate.
Magnesium: Commonly deficient. Magnesium glycinate is well-tolerated form.
Probiotics: Beneficial bacteria for gut health. Found in yogurt, kefir, kimchi, or supplements.
Creatine: Safe and effective for muscle performance. 3-5g creatine monohydrate daily.
Protein powder: Convenient when dietary protein is insufficient. Whey, casein, or plant-based options.
